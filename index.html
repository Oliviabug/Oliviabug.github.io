CHINA IS STILL A TOY-MANUFACTURING POWERHOUSE

Western toymakers hope the country’s growing middle class will boost sales, too

ON DECEMBER 25th children across the globe awoke to find toys stuffed into stockings and crammed beneath Christmas trees. Parents will insist that these goodies were built by elves and delivered by a bearded man in a red suit. The truth, of course, is not so magical. The world’s real toy workshop is not at the North Pole but in China. Today, three-quarters of all toys and games are made by the country’s 10,000 toy manufacturers. According to the United Nations, China exported $44bn-worth of toys in 2016, with $25bn-worth going to America, $4bn-worth to Japan and another $4bn-worth to Britain.

Although China has dominated toy manufacturing for decades, market pressures are growing. Rising wages are squeezing profits, prompting some toymakers to move production to India or Vietnam, where labour is cheaper...
